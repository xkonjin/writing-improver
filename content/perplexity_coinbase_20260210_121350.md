### Ad Description and Intent
Coinbase aired a 60-second minimalist spot during the second quarter of Super Bowl 60 (February 8, 2026, at Levi's Stadium), showing scrolling karaoke lyrics to "Everybody" with simple animations resembling early computer graphics, ending with "Crypto is for **Everybody**" and claims like "Am I the original crypto exchange? ### Context and "Backstreet Bowl" Competition
The ad overlapped with T-Mobile's flamboyant "I Want It That Way" spot (same quarter, with Backstreet Boys performances and celebs like Machine Gun Kelly), sparking a licensing "bidding war" that Coinbase won for "Everybody."[1] Both brands viewed the Backstreet Boys' cross-generational appeal positively; Coinbase wasn't deterred by T-Mobile's plans and saw comparisons as beneficial chatter.[1] Industry insiders called it surprising ("not on my Super Bowl bingo card"), but many creatives preferred Coinbase's: Annika Welander (Someoddpilot CEO) praised it as punching "out of its weight class," while Amir Sahba (Thinkingbox CEO) found it confusing vs. T-Mobile's emotional pull.[1]
