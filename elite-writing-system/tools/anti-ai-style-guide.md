# The Anti-AI Style Guide

## How to Write Like a Human in 2025

AI writing is everywhere. This guide helps you maintain distinctive human voice and avoid the telltale patterns that make writing feel artificial.

---

## Part 1: The AI Writing Epidemic

### What Makes Writing Feel Like AI?

#### The Deadly Sins of AI Writing

1. **Uniform Sentence Length**: Every sentence 15-20 words
2. **Transition Addiction**: Furthermore, Moreover, Additionally in every paragraph
3. **Hedge Word Overdose**: Perhaps, maybe, potentially, arguably
4. **Perfect Consistency**: Same tone throughout entire piece
5. **Generic Examples**: "A company" instead of "When Airbnb..."
6. **Balanced Everything**: Always showing both sides equally
7. **Conclusion Repetition**: Ending that just restates beginning

### The AI Fingerprint (2025 Edition)

#### Words AI Overuses

- Delve
- Crucial
- Comprehensive
- Landscape (as in "digital landscape")
- Leverage (as verb)
- Utilize
- Paradigm
- Synergy
- Holistic
- Robust

#### Phrases That Scream AI

- "It's important to note that..."
- "In today's fast-paced world..."
- "At the end of the day..."
- "Let's dive into..."
- "On the flip side..."
- "To put it simply..."
- "In conclusion..."
- "As we navigate..."
- "The bottom line is..."
- "It goes without saying..."

## Part 2: The Human Advantage

### What AI Can't Do (Yet)

#### 1. Personal Experience

AI has no memories, no embarrassments, no victories. It can't tell you about the time it failed spectacularly or succeeded unexpectedly.

**AI Version**: "Failure often teaches valuable lessons."
**Human Version**: "I lost $50K on my first startup because I built features nobody wanted."

#### 2. Emotional Variability

Humans get angry, excited, bored, passionate—sometimes in the same paragraph.

**AI Version**: Maintains steady, professional tone
**Human Version**: Starts calm, builds to rant, ends with humor

#### 3. Specific References

AI generalizes. Humans specify.

**AI Version**: "Many experts believe..."
**Human Version**: "Patrick Collison told me at a dinner in 2019..."

#### 4. Imperfection

Humans make interesting mistakes, use colloquialisms, break rules deliberately.

**AI Version**: Grammatically perfect always
**Human Version**: Fragments. For emphasis. Breaking rules because it works.

#### 5. Genuine Controversy

AI hedges. Humans take stands.

**AI Version**: "There are various perspectives on this issue..."
**Human Version**: "This is wrong and here's why everyone else missed it."

## Part 3: Techniques for Human Writing

### Rhythm and Variation

#### Sentence Length Patterns

**AI Pattern**:
15 words. 18 words. 16 words. 17 words.

**Human Pattern**:
Two words. Then a really long sentence that goes on for thirty or forty words, building complexity and adding nuance before suddenly stopping. Short punch. Medium elaboration with specific details. Another tiny fragment.

#### The 2-35-5 Rule

- Some sentences: 2 words
- Some sentences: 35+ words
- Most sentences: 5-15 words
- Never let 3 consecutive sentences have similar length

### Voice Injection Techniques

#### 1. The Aside

Use parentheticals (like this one) to add personality. Dashes work too—especially for emphasis.

#### 2. Direct Address

Stop explaining. Start talking to the reader. You know what I mean. See how this feels different?

#### 3. Question Breaks

Why do this? Because it creates dialogue. Doesn't it feel more engaging? Of course it does.

#### 4. The Incomplete Thought

Sometimes you just...
Let things trail off.
Start new paragraphs mid-thought.

#### 5. Emotional Punctuation

Really? REALLY?! Come on.
This. Changes. Everything.

### Anti-Pattern Replacements

#### Instead of Hedging, Be Direct

**AI**: "It could be argued that perhaps this might be important"
**Human**: "This matters. Here's why:"

#### Instead of Transitions, Jump

**AI**: "Furthermore, we should also consider..."
**Human**: "Another thing—" or just start the next point

#### Instead of Balance, Take Sides

**AI**: "While there are merits to both perspectives..."
**Human**: "The first approach works. The second doesn't. Let me explain."

#### Instead of Summarizing, End Sharp

**AI**: "In conclusion, as we've discussed..."
**Human**: "That's it. That's the whole thing." or "Now go do something about it."

## Part 4: The Humanity Checklist

### Before Publishing, Check:

#### Structure & Flow

- [ ] Sentence lengths vary by 10+ words
- [ ] At least one single-word sentence
- [ ] At least one 40+ word sentence
- [ ] Paragraph lengths inconsistent
- [ ] No formulaic transitions
- [ ] Unexpected section breaks

#### Voice & Personality

- [ ] Contains personal anecdote
- [ ] Includes specific names/companies/dates
- [ ] Shows clear opinion/bias
- [ ] Has emotional variation
- [ ] Contains imperfect moment
- [ ] Uses unexpected analogy

#### Language & Style

- [ ] Zero use of AI favorite words
- [ ] Includes colloquialisms
- [ ] Has conversational moments
- [ ] Contains rhetorical questions
- [ ] Uses fragments deliberately
- [ ] Includes surprising word choice

#### Content & Substance

- [ ] Makes controversial claim
- [ ] Cites specific source
- [ ] Includes precise numbers
- [ ] Has unique insight
- [ ] Tells actual story
- [ ] Provides unexpected example

## Part 5: Advanced Human Techniques

### The Signature Move

Develop 2-3 unique stylistic choices that become your trademark:

- Always starting with dialogue
- Using specific musical references
- Mathematical analogies for business concepts
- Historical parallels to modern problems
- Pop culture metaphors for technical concepts

### The Vulnerability Play

Share something AI never would:

- A costly mistake you made
- An embarrassing misunderstanding
- A belief you changed your mind about
- A time you were completely wrong
- A fear you still have

### The Specificity Weapon

Replace every generic reference:

- "A study shows" → "MIT's 2024 analysis of 10,000 startups"
- "Many people" → "73% of developers I surveyed"
- "Recently" → "Last Tuesday at 3pm"
- "Some companies" → "Stripe, Discord, and Linear"
- "Experts say" → "Daniel Kahneman argues in Thinking Fast and Slow"

### The Opinion Bomb

State something boldly without hedging:

- "This will fail."
- "Everyone is wrong about this."
- "The solution is obvious."
- "This changes everything."
- "Nothing else matters."

### The Pattern Break

Deliberately violate expectations:

- Start with the conclusion
- End mid-thought
- Change tone suddenly
- Insert random observation
- Break your own rule

## Part 6: Emergency Protocols

### If You Must Use AI

#### The Humanization Process

1. **Generate draft with AI**
2. **Rewrite first sentence completely**
3. **Add personal example to paragraph 2**
4. **Insert controversial opinion**
5. **Vary every sentence length**
6. **Remove all hedge words**
7. **Add specific numbers/names**
8. **Include one mistake/admission**
9. **Rewrite conclusion as call to action**
10. **Read aloud and fix robot rhythms**

#### The 40% Rule

If AI writes it, you must:

- Change 40% of words
- Restructure 40% of sentences
- Add 40% new content
- Delete 40% of transitions

### Detection Evasion

If you're being checked:

1. Write first paragraph from scratch
2. Use personal pronouns freely
3. Include specific date/location
4. Add emotional variation
5. Break at least three grammar rules
6. Include niche reference
7. State strong opinion
8. Use unexpected punctuation
9. Vary paragraph lengths wildly
10. End unexpectedly

---

## The Ultimate Test

Read your writing aloud. If it sounds like:

- A real person talking = Success
- A corporate press release = AI detected
- A textbook = Too formal
- A conversation = Perfect

The best anti-AI technique?

Have something to say that only you could say.

Write from experience. Write with conviction. Write like you talk when you're excited about something.

Be weird. Be specific. Be wrong sometimes.

Be human.

That's it. No summary needed. You get it.
