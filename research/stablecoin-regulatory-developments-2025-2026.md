## 1. GENIUS Act Status

**Status:** Signed into law July 18, 2025

**Vote breakdown:**

- Senate: 68-30 (June 17, 2025)
- House: 308-122 (July 17, 2025)
- President Trump signed July 18, 2025

**Key provisions:**

- 1:1 backing requirement for payment stablecoins (USD or low-risk assets)
- Insured depository institutions subject to oversight by primary financial regulator
- Federally licensed nonbank issuers subject to OCC oversight
- Payment stablecoins excluded from "security" (SEC) and "commodity" (CFTC) definitions
- Only permitted payment stablecoin issuers can issue in US
- Implementing rules deadline: July 18, 2026
- State regulatory regime certification deadline: July 18, 2026 (via Stablecoin Regulatory Coordination Council)

**Sources:**

- [Latham & Watkins: GENIUS Act Adoption](https://www.lw.com/en/insights/the-genius-act-of-2025-stablecoin-legislation-adopted-in-the-us)

## 2. State-Level Stablecoin Regulations

### Wyoming

**Framework:**

- Wyoming Stable Token Commission established
- Launched Frontier Stable Token (FRNT): August 20, 2025
- First state-issued stablecoin in US
- Public sales began: January 7, 2026
- $1.5 million sold through January 13, 2026

**Technical details:**

- 1:1 redeemable for USD
- 2% statutory overcollateralization buffer
- Reserve assets held in trust
- Deployed on Solana blockchain
- Available via Kraken
- Transaction fees: <$0.01
- Settlement time: seconds
- Reserve manager: Franklin Templeton
- Custodian: Fiduciary Trust Company International
- Interest from reserves directed to Wyoming school program

**Sources:**

- [Wyoming Public Media: FRNT Public Purchase](https://www.wyomingpublicmedia.org/wyoming-economy/2026-01-07/wyoming-releases-frnt-stablecoin-for-public-purchase)
- [Payments Dive: Wyoming Plans](https://www.paymentsdive.com/news/wyoming-has-big-plans-for-stablecoin/810103/)
- [Ledger Insights: FRNT Launch](https://www.ledgerinsights.com/wyomings-frnt-stablecoin-is-live-franklin-templeton-manages-reserves/)

### New York

**Requirements (existing framework, clarified 2022):**

- BitLicense required since 2015
- Stablecoin issuance approved since 2018
- Full reserves in highly safe and liquid assets required
- Monthly attestations by independent accountants
- Timely redemption under clearly disclosed policies

**Sources:**

- [Morgan Lewis: Federal vs State Divide](https://www.morganlewis.com/pubs/2025/09/the-genius-acts-stablecoin-regulatory-scheme-promotes-uniformity-but-may-fall-short)

### General Federal Requirements (GENIUS Act)

- 1:1 backing with cash or Treasury securities
- Monthly reserve composition reporting
- Federal anti-money laundering regulations apply
- No interest payments allowed
- Must be redeemable for fixed monetary value

## 3. MiCA Enforcement in Europe

### Tether (USDT) Delisting

**Timeline:**

- MiCA stablecoin rules enforceable: March 31, 2025
- July 1, 2025: USDT banned from trading on European exchanges and company-to-company transactions

**Exchange actions:**

- **Coinbase Europe:** Delisted USDT December 2024
- **Crypto.com:** Stopped offering USDT January 31, 2025; auto-conversion deadline March 31, 2025
- **Binance:** Announced delisting March 2025; effective March 31, 2025 at 23:59 UTC
  - 9 stablecoins delisted including USDT
  - USDT still available for custody, withdrawal, deposit
  - USDT still tradable in perpetual contracts for EEA users
- **Kraken:** Sell-only mode March 24; full trading disabled March 31, 2025

**Reason:** Tether has not pursued MiCA compliance. No licensed entity authorized to issue USDT in EU.

**Sources:**

## 7. Regulatory Enforcement Actions

### Overall Shift: From Enforcement to Framework

**April 2025:** Department of Justice memo marked shift away from "regulation by prosecution"

- Disbanded National Cryptocurrency Enforcement Team
- Wound down cases imposing regulatory frameworks
- Refocused on underlying crimes: terrorism, narcotics, sanctions evasion

**SEC actions:**

- Dropped nearly all Biden-era enforcement actions against fintechs
- Actions dropped: unregistered broker-dealer, issuance, exchange, clearing agency allegations without fraud
- Shift toward establishing clear frameworks vs enforcement

**CFTC guidance:**

- Issued no-action relief for FCMs to accept non-securities digital assets (including payment stablecoins) as customer collateral

**Joint regulatory effort:**

- September 2025: SEC and CFTC joint statement launching cross-agency initiative for blockchain regulatory clarity

**No major enforcement actions against stablecoin issuers since mid-2025**

**Sources:**

- [Morgan Lewis: CFTC Guidance Overhaul](https://www.morganlewis.com/pubs/2025/12/us-regulatory-crypto-sprint-continues-as-cftc-overhauls-guidance-on-digital-assets)

## 8. International Developments

### Hong Kong

**Framework:** Stablecoin Ordinance effective August 2025

**Requirements:**

- HKMA license required for issuance, marketing, distribution of fiat-backed stablecoins to Hong Kong public
- Foreign issuers of HKD-pegged tokens must hold HKMA license
- Sandbox for supervised testing before full authorization
- First batch of licenses: Expected early 2026

**Sources:**

- [BVNK: Global Stablecoin Regulations 2026](https://bvnk.com/blog/global-stablecoin-regulations-2026)
- [XREX: Stablecoins in 2026](https://xrex.io/blog/industry-trends/stablecoins-in-2026-regulatory-landscapes-in-singapore-japan-taiwan-south-korea-europe-post-the-us-genius-act/)

### United Arab Emirates

**Framework:** Central Bank Payment Token Services Regulation effective August 2024

**Requirements:**

- CBUAE license required for issuance, redemption, facilitation of payment tokens in UAE mainland
- Payment tokens defined as: crypto assets fully backed by one or more fiat currencies used for settlement/transfers
- 1:1 fiat reserve backing mandatory
- Redemption at par value

- [BVNK: Global Stablecoin Regulations 2026](https://bvnk.com/blog/global-stablecoin-regulations-2026)
- [Sumsub: Global Stablecoin Compliance](https://sumsub.com/blog/global-stablecoin-compliance-guide/)

### Japan

**Sources:**

### South Korea

**Framework:** Digital Asset Basic Act

**Timeline:**

- Expected passage: Q1 2026
- Delayed from 2025 due to regulatory turf battle

**Regulatory dispute:**

- **Bank of Korea position:** Only bank-led consortiums with 51% ownership can issue stablecoins
- **Financial Services Commission position:** More flexible approach; warns 51% rule could block fintech competition

**Key provisions:**

- First authorization of domestic KRW-backed stablecoin issuance (prohibited for 9 years)
- Reserve requirement: >100% of circulating supply
- Reserves held at banks/approved institutions, separated from issuer balance sheet
- Minimum capital: 5 billion KRW (~$3.5 million USD)
- No-fault liability: Operators responsible for user losses without proven negligence

**Sources:**

- [KoreaTechDesk: Korea Stablecoin Moment](https://koreatechdesk.com/korea-stablecoin-digital-asset-act-fintech-banks)
- [CoinDesk: South Korea Law Stalls](https://www.coindesk.com/policy/2025/12/30/south-korea-s-long-awaited-crypto-law-stalls-over-who-can-issue-stablecoins)
- [Crypto News: South Korea Postpones](https://crypto.news/south-korea-postpones-digital-asset-law-to-2026/)

### Common Global Principles

Across Hong Kong, UAE, Singapore, Europe (MiCA):

- Mandatory licensing under financial supervision
- AML/KYC controls required
- 1:1 fiat reserve backing
- Redemption at par value for holders
