## 7. Banking-as-a-Service for Crypto & Operation Chokepoint 2.0

### Operation Chokepoint 2.0: Definition

- **What**: Biden-era initiative where federal regulators encouraged banking institutions to sever ties with crypto firms
- **Impact**: Account closures, service refusals, warning letters to dozens of institutions
- **Origin**: Revived playbook from Obama-era Operation Chokepoint (targeted "risky" industries)
- **Source**: [Congress.gov Library of Congress](https://www.congress.gov/crs-product/R48430), [Consumer Financial Services Law Monitor](https://www.consumerfinancialserviceslawmonitor.com/2025/12/from-operation-chokepoint-2-0-to-fair-banking-what-the-house-report-alleges-and-how-the-occ-responded/)

### Regulatory Changes in 2025

#### Presidential Action

- **Date**: January 2025
- **Action**: President Trump rescinded Biden-era executive order, issued new one supporting digital asset growth and use
- **Source**: [American Banker](https://www.americanbanker.com/news/banks-could-see-an-end-to-chokepoint-2-0-in-2025)

#### OCC (Office of the Comptroller of the Currency)

- **Action**: Approved national trust charter applications for five crypto firms (date not specified, but 2025)
- **Policy**: Affirmed administration's push to allow crypto companies to take deposits
- **Permitted activities**:
  - Crypto custody
  - Certain stablecoin activities
  - Participation in distributed ledgers (when conducted safely and soundly)
- **Prior actions rescinded**: Prior interpretive letters
- **Source**: [Consumer Financial Services Law Monitor](https://www.consumerfinancialserviceslawmonitor.com/2025/12/from-operation-chokepoint-2-0-to-fair-banking-what-the-house-report-alleges-and-how-the-occ-responded/), [Davis Wright Tremaine](https://www.dwt.com/blogs/financial-services-law-advisor/2024/12/debanking-crypto-regulation-trump-andreessen-sacks)

#### Federal Reserve

- **Actions**:
  - Withdrew prior supervisory letters
  - Sunsetted Novel Activities Supervision Program
  - Removed "reputational risk" from examination programs
- **Source**: [Consumer Financial Services Law Monitor](https://www.consumerfinancialserviceslawmonitor.com/2025/12/from-operation-chokepoint-2-0-to-fair-banking-what-the-house-report-alleges-and-how-the-occ-responded/)

- **Source**: [Consumer Financial Services Law Monitor](https://www.consumerfinancialserviceslawmonitor.com/2025/12/from-operation-chokepoint-2-0-to-fair-banking-what-the-house-report-alleges-and-how-the-occ-responded/)

## 8. Tokenized Deposits vs. Stablecoins

### Structural Differences

#### Balance Sheet Treatment

- **Stablecoins**: NOT held on bank's balance sheet; represent 1:1 reserve of fiat currency
- **Tokenized deposits**: Held ON bank's balance sheet
- **Source**: [Finovate](https://finovate.com/tokenized-deposits-vs-stablecoins-whats-the-difference-and-why-it-matters/), [Spydra](https://www.spydra.app/blog/tokenized-deposits-vs-stablecoins-financial-institutions)

#### Issuer

- **Stablecoins**: Issued by private, non-bank companies (most)
- **Tokenized deposits**: Issued by banks, usually used internally for payment settlement
- **Source**: [Finovate](https://finovate.com/tokenized-deposits-vs-stablecoins-whats-the-difference-and-why-it-matters/)

#### Regulatory Framework

- **Stablecoins**: Varied rules across regions
  - MiCA (EU)
  - Payment Services Act (Singapore)
  - Evolving frameworks (U.S.)
- **Tokenized deposits**: Fall under existing banking regulations
  - Inherit all existing banking compliance frameworks (KYC/AML, capital requirements)
- **Source**: [Finovate](https://finovate.com/tokenized-deposits-vs-stablecoins-whats-the-difference-and-why-it-matters/), [CU Management](https://www.cumanagement.com/articles/2025/08/how-stablecoins-and-tokenized-deposits-could-reshape-us-financial-services)

#### Liquidity Effects

- **Stablecoins**: Remove liquidity
  - When consumers convert fiat to stablecoins, fiat sits in reserves as safe passive assets
  - Reduces money multiplier effect
- **Tokenized deposits**: Maintain liquidity
  - Stay on bank's balance sheet
  - Funds usable for lending, investing, general liquidity management
- **Source**: [Federal Reserve FEDS Notes](https://www.federalreserve.gov/econres/notes/feds-notes/banks-in-the-age-of-stablecoins-implications-for-deposits-credit-and-financial-intermediation-20251217.html), [Spydra](https://www.spydra.app/blog/tokenized-deposits-vs-stablecoins-financial-institutions)

#### Use Cases

- **Stablecoins**:
  - Cross-border payments
  - Programmable payments
  - DeFi
- **Tokenized deposits**:
  - Domestic real-time payments
  - B2B payments
  - Treasury automation
- **Source**: [Finovate](https://finovate.com/tokenized-deposits-vs-stablecoins-whats-the-difference-and-why-it-matters/)

### 2026 Market Trajectory

- **Prediction**: Tokenized bank deposits expected to overtake stablecoins as preferred on-chain dollar for institutional and wholesale money
- **Reason**: Banks turn deposits into programmable infrastructure without breaking existing financial system
- **Clarification**: Stablecoins don't disappear, but institutional preference shifts
