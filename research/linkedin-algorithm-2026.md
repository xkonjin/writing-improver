## 1. ALGORITHM MECHANICS (2025-2026)

### Core Ranking System

**Three-Stage Filter**:
1. Quality filtering (AI spam detection, engagement bait detection)
2. Small-audience testing (first-hour performance to connections)
3. Broader relevance scoring (2nd/3rd-degree distribution if engagement hits threshold)

**Ranking Formula**: Relevance + Expertise + Clarity + Early Engagement (not recency)

### Top Ranking Signals (Weighted Order)

| Signal | Weight | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| **Dwell Time** | Highest | 61+ seconds = 15.6% engagement rate; <3 seconds = 1.2% |
| **Expert Comments** | 5-7x | Comments from industry experts weighted 5-7x vs random connections |
| **Discussion Threads** | 5.2x | Back-and-forth conversations = 5.2x amplification vs single comments |
| **First-Hour Engagement** | Critical | Determines 2nd/3rd-degree distribution |
| **Comment Quality** | High | Thoughtful comments >>> generic "Great post!" |
| **Shares** | High | Algorithm detects value-adds vs vanity shares |
| **Reactions** | Medium | Lower weight than comments/shares |

### Engagement Bait Detection (2026)

LinkedIn now penalizes:
- "Comment YES if you agree"
- "Tag someone who needs this"
- "Share if this resonates"
- Giveaway-style engagement farming

**Workaround**: Ask genuine questions that require domain expertise to answer.

### Native Content Bias

LinkedIn strongly favors native formats:
- Text posts
- PDF/document carousels
- Native video
- Native articles

External links trigger 25-60% reach reduction (see Section 9).

### PDF Carousel Technical Specs

**Current Status** (2026):
- Native carousel feature removed in late 2023

- Supports PDF, PPTX, DOCX (PDF recommended for consistency)

**Optimal Specs**:
- **Dimensions**: 1080x1080 (1:1) or 1080x1350 (4:5 portrait)
- **File Size**: Up to 100MB, 300 pages max
- **Ideal Length**: 6-10 slides (drop-off after 10 unless high value/slide)
- **Text**: 10-20 words/slide, headings 40-60pt, body 24-36pt
- **First Slide**: Must act as thumbnailâ€”massive headline + arrow pointing right

**Tools**: Canva (templates), Figma (custom/branded)

**Content Types for Carousels**:
- Frameworks (e.g., "3-Stage Stablecoin Adoption Curve")
- Data visualizations (e.g., "Crypto Regulation Timeline 2025-2026")

- Before/After comparisons
- Myth-busting (e.g., "5 Things TradFi Gets Wrong About Stablecoins")

### Video Performance Decline

**2025 Data**: Video engagement crashed 35% year-over-year.

**Why**:
- Viewer retention drops after 30 seconds
- Only 18% continue past 1 minute
- Most users watch on mute (captions required)

**When to Use Video**:
- <60 seconds only
- Caption-first (don't rely on audio)
- First 3 seconds must hook hard
- Use for personality/authenticity, not information density

## 4. TAGGING STRATEGY

### The Data

| Tagging Approach | Reach Impact | Algorithm Response |
|------------------|--------------|---------------------|
| **3-5 relevant tags** | +20% reach | Optimal |
| **Strategic tagging (engagement expected)** | +80% if tagged person responds in 4 hours | 1.8x impact multiplier |
| **10+ tags** | Algorithm penalty | Flagged as spam |
| **Unrelated tags** | Suppression | Detected as manipulation |

### Best Practices

**When to Tag**:
1. Tagging someone whose work you're citing/referencing
2. Tagging collaborators or interview subjects
3. Tagging 1-2 experts likely to engage (quality > quantity)

**Where to Tag**:
- **Post body**: If central to content (e.g., "This framework from @expert...")
- **First comment**: If supplementary (e.g., "h/t @person for the data")

**Never Tag**:
- Random influencers hoping they'll engage
- More than 5 people total
- Companies/brands unless directly relevant to post

### Expert Tag Multiplier

Comments from tagged industry experts = 7-9x algorithmic weight vs random connection comments.

**Implication**: One thoughtful comment from a fintech VP beats 10 generic comments from unrelated connections.

- Only first ~2 lines visible before "...see more" fold
- 80% of users decide to expand based on hook alone

### Hook Writing Best Practices

**Structure**:
- Line 1: Attention grab (bold claim, surprising fact, or question)
- Line 2: Promise of value or tease the insight

**Formatting**:

- Break into 2-3 lines visually (not one long sentence)
- Avoid fluff words ("I've been thinking," "Recently, I realized")

**Voice**:
- Confident but not arrogant
- Specific (not vague platitudes)
- Domain expertise signals ("I analyzed 200 deals," "After 5 years in TradFi")

## 9. LINKEDIN ARTICLE SEO

### Do LinkedIn Articles Rank in Google?

**Yes**. LinkedIn articles can rank in Google search results due to:
1. **LinkedIn's domain authority** (very high DA)
2. **SEO-friendly article editor** (custom meta titles/descriptions)
3. **Public indexing** (articles are crawled by Google)

### Optimization Strategy

#### On-Page SEO for LinkedIn Articles

**Title**:
- Front-load primary keyword
- Keep under 60 characters (Google truncates after)
- Example: "Stablecoin Regulation 2026: What the GENIUS Act Means for Payments"

**Custom SEO Title/Description**:
- LinkedIn lets you set custom meta title + description in article editor
- Use this for target keywords (distinct from display title)

**Headings**:
- Use H2/H3 structure (LinkedIn article editor supports)
- Include semantic keywords in subheadings
- Example H2: "How Federal Stablecoin Oversight Changes Crypto Payments"

**Keyword Placement**:
- First 100 words (critical for Google)
- Subheadings
- Naturally throughout (avoid stuffing)

**Internal/External Links**:
- Link to other LinkedIn articles (builds internal link graph)
- Link to credible external sources (signals quality)

#### Keyword Research for Finance/Crypto

**Use SEMrush, Ahrefs, or Google Keyword Planner**:
- Target long-tail keywords (less competition)
- Focus on informational intent ("what is," "how does," "why are")
- Example keywords:
  - "stablecoin regulation 2026"
  - "GENIUS Act stablecoins"
  - "institutional crypto adoption"
  - "crypto payments infrastructure"

### Google Ranking Factors (2026)

**E-E-A-T** (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness):
- Your LinkedIn profile = author credibility signal
- Complete profile with finance/crypto experience boosts article ranking
- LinkedIn's domain authority carries weight

**Content Quality**:
- Depth (800-1200 words optimal)
- Original insights (not rehashed content)
- Data/citations (increases trustworthiness)

### LinkedIn Articles vs Posts (SEO Perspective)

| Factor | LinkedIn Article | LinkedIn Post |
|--------|------------------|---------------|
| **Google Indexing** | Full text indexed | Partial/inconsistent |
| **URL Structure** | Clean, article-specific | Generic post ID |
| **Longevity** | Evergreen (months/years) | Feed-driven (days/weeks) |
| **SEO Control** | Custom meta tags | Limited |
| **Backlink Potential** | Higher (shareable URL) | Lower |

**Recommendation**: For evergreen thought leadership (e.g., "The Ultimate Guide to Stablecoin Types"), use LinkedIn Articles. For timely thesis content, use posts/carousels.
